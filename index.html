01 The Idea

Belief is a verb.

Most groups stop at the conversation. We start there and then we make something. Faith without works is dead — so we treat faith like source code: it has to compile, it has to run, it has to serve someone.

This is for builders. Founders, makers, dads, dreamers. Men who are vibe-coding a product, leading a team, mending a marriage, or just trying to take the next faithful step. We seed faith directly into what we ship: products, services, and community. Less theory. More action. Take the leap.

// James 2:26 — the algorithm
while (faith.alive) {
  pray();
  build("something that serves");
  ship(); // faith, with works
}

// the whole point
const faith = works > 0
  ? "alive"
  : "dead";

02 The Table

We break bread first.

Before we plan or build anything, we eat. Real bread, simple ingredients, a shared cup. The oldest meal in the book — and the place God keeps meeting men.

Our bread comes from the same line: Communion Breads — slow-fermented, hand-shaped, in the pattern of Melchizedek's loaf and Ezekiel's six simple grains. Simple things that multiply, like five loaves in tired hands.

The bread story →
"Then Melchizedek, king of Salem, brought out bread and wine. He was priest of God Most High, and he blessed Abram."

Genesis 14:18–19

  • CommunionThe cup and the loaf. We remember Him together before we do anything else.
  • Ezekiel's sixWheat, barley, beans, lentils, millet, spelt — bread baked by command (Ezekiel 4:9).
  • The multiplyingA small meal, simple ingredients, more than enough. Five loaves, twelve baskets left.

03 The Night

Four movements, every Wednesday.

A simple, repeatable rhythm. Come as you are — there's a seat and a slice waiting.

01 — Gather

Pull up a chair

Doors open, worship music playing, the table's set. We catch up on the blessings and what we're grateful for.

02 — Break bread

The meal & the cup

We break bread and take communion. A shared loaf, God stories, a blessing before the Bible study.

03 — Open the Word

A short study

One chapter together. We divide up the reading and discuss as a group.

04 — Build & send

Faith, with works

We name one action each — something to build, mend, or ship this week. Then we pray it forward and go do it.

Men who built.

The Bible is full of makers. We're standing in a long line of them.

Bezalel
The Spirit-filled craftsman

"Filled with the Spirit of God, with skill, ability and knowledge in all kinds of crafts" to build the tabernacle. Faith expressed as craft. (Exodus 31)

Nehemiah
The rebuilder

Prayed, then picked up a trowel. Rebuilt a city wall in 52 days with a tool in one hand and a sword in the other. Faith that shows up to work. (Nehemiah 6)

The boy with the loaves
The small offering

Five loaves and two fish, handed over. In the right hands, a small thing fed thousands. Bring what you have. (John 6)
